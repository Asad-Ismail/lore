# Huggingface

## Definition

Huggingface is a platform and ecosystem for natural language processing (NLP) and machine learning (ML) research, development, and deployment. It provides a wide range of tools, libraries, and pre-trained models for tasks such as text classification, named entity recognition, and text generation. Huggingface is particularly known for its Huggingface Transformers library, which offers a unified interface for working with a variety of pre-trained models.

## Context

Huggingface was founded in 2018 by Thomas Wolf and others as a community-driven project aimed at making NLP research more accessible. The platform has grown to become one of the most important resources for NLP researchers and developers. Huggingface provides a variety of tools and resources, including pre-trained models, training code, and inference tools. It also provides a repository for sharing and discovering models, as well as a platform for benchmarking and evaluating models.

## Connections

- Huggingface is closely related to the field of NLP and ML, particularly in the area of pre-trained models.
- The Huggingface Transformers library is widely used in the NLP community for its ease of use and extensive range of pre-trained models.
- Huggingface is also related to the broader ML community, as it provides tools and resources for a variety of ML tasks.
- Huggingface is part of the broader open-source community, and its platform is built on the principles of open-source development.
